01 What reconciliation actually is

One real money event shows up in several systems — and they don't always agree by themselves. A €1,000 client deposit appears at the payment provider ("we received €1,000"), in the CRM ("deposit approved"), and in cTrader ("balance credited"). Reconciliation is simply checking that they agree — and surfacing every case where they don't.

Same event. Different systems. Same truth.

When they disagree, that's a break (an exception). The dangerous ones are obvious once you see them: client paid but never credited (an angry client, money in limbo) or credited but never paid (the company just gave money away). The whole machine is five steps:

sources→match→breaks→review→report & audit

The forex vocabulary is just the skin. Underneath, it's a systems problem: pull data from multiple sources, normalize it, match it, detect the exceptions, and give finance a clean trail to resolve and audit.

02 What the demo does

It takes three exports — payment provider, CRM, cTrader — and runs a real matching cascade: exact reference, then a tolerant match (small fee / FX / settlement-timing differences), then a fuzzy match, and whatever's left becomes a typed exception. You get a one-screen summary, a filterable list, and a click-in view that shows all three systems side by side with the reason and a suggested next action. Nothing is uploaded anywhere — it all runs in the browser.

03 The data the demo expects

So you can see exactly what's going in, the demo reads three CSV exports with these shapes (the sample set follows them). It's already tolerant of real-world mess — comma or semicolon files, European or US number formats (1.234,56 or 1,234.56), different date formats, and differently-named columns all mapped to one common model:

Payment provider
psp_txn_id, payment_ref, sender_name, account, value, ccy, type, processed_at, state
CRM / back-office
crm_ref, client_name, login, txn_type, amount, currency, value_date, fx_rate, status
cTrader balance operations
balance_id, ctid_account, operation, delta_cents, currency, server_time, comment

The match keys are the shared payment reference, then amount + account + currency + value-date when no single reference exists.

04 · The real build works the other way around

You don't adapt to the demo — the system adapts to you. In production you give us your actual exports, in whatever format your systems already produce. We map your real data, define the matching rules that fit how you reconcile today, and then optimize that process inside the system.

We don't make you change how you work. We encode how you work — and make it faster, safer, and continuous: "show us your systems, your exports, and what you check by hand today. We map the flow, define the matching rules, and build the tool that finds the mismatches."

The path is deliberately de-risked: discovery (map your real flow) → MVP on your worst stream, proving value on your own data → expand the other streams → optimize (the system keeps learning your patterns, so the breaks queue shrinks over time).

05 Core now, optional later — confirmed with you

What you see running is the core: sources → match → breaks → review → report → audit. Everything beyond it — exposure / risk reconciliation, client-money segregation reporting, IB & partner commissions, bundled audit packs — is a possible module, marked clearly as to be confirmed against your workflow. We'd rather map your real process than show you numbers we invented. See the dashboard for the full map, and the glossary for every term in plain English.

06 What we'd need from you to scope it

  • Platform — cTrader confirmed? Open API / a read-only DB replica?
  • Funding rails — which PSPs / banks / crypto for deposits & withdrawals?
  • Bank statements — format (MT940 / camt.053 / CSV) and how they're delivered?
  • CRM + accounting / GL — which systems, and read access to each?
  • Today — what do you reconcile now, and how? Which streams are worst?
  • The trigger — a loss, a near-miss, an audit, a failed close, or scaling?
  • Scale — funding transactions & trading accounts per day, number of currencies?
  • Delivery — cloud or on-prem / private? Who operates it (finance / back-office / risk)?

With those answered and a couple of sample exports, we define the exact MVP scope, the timeline, and a precise quote.
