Glossary — every term, in plain English

A reconciliation system uses a lot of finance vocabulary. Here is every word that appears in the demo, explained simply — so the meeting is a conversation, not a vocabulary test.

The honest split. Core = the reconciliation machine, shown working live. Advanced = possible modules — real, but to be confirmed against your actual workflow before we build them. We'd rather map your real process than show numbers we invented.

CORE the honest machine — shown working live

Reconciliation
Proving that two independent records of the same money agree. That's the whole product.Proving the same payment agrees across your systems.
Source
One system that recorded the money. The demo uses three: the payment provider, the CRM, and cTrader.One system that recorded the money.
PSP / Payment provider
The rail client money arrives and leaves on — cards, e-wallets (Skrill, Neteller), bank transfers.Payment service provider — the rail money flows in and out on.
CRM / back-office
The broker's system of record for clients: deposit requests, withdrawal requests, onboarding, statuses.Your back-office record of every client deposit and withdrawal.
cTrader
The trading platform TopFX runs (by Spotware) — where client balances are credited and debited. (cTrader's equivalent of MT4/MT5.)The trading platform — where client balances are credited.
Source pair
Two systems being compared against each other — e.g. payment provider ↔ cTrader, or CRM ↔ bank.The two systems being compared.
Match / Matched
The same transaction was found in every source and the figures agree. Safe — no action.Found in every system and the figures agree.
Break a.k.a. Exception
A mismatch between records that a human must explain. "Break" and "exception" mean the same thing — both are standard; the demo uses "exception."A mismatch the system caught and sent to a human.
Unmatched
A record that exists in one system with no matching record in the others. The most important class — it's where "paid but not credited" and "credited but never paid" live.Exists in one system, missing in the others.
Duplicate
The same payment recorded or processed more than once (often a double-click or a webhook retry).The same payment processed twice.
Tolerance
A match where the amounts differ by a tiny, expected amount — a processing fee or FX rounding — close enough to count as agreed. The exact thresholds are set with you, not assumed.A small allowed difference so a fee or 1-cent gap isn't flagged.
Needs review
The reference ties the records together but the amounts disagree by more than a fee — a person should check it.Reference matches but amounts don't — needs a human.
Suspicious (possible loss)
A pattern that may be a real loss or error — for example a payout sent twice against one authorised request.A pattern that may be a real loss — flagged for urgent review.
Reference / exact match
The shared ID both systems carry for the same payment — the primary key used to tie records together.The shared ID used to tie two records together.
Composite match
When there's no single shared reference, matching on a combination of fields instead — account + amount + date. The demo does this for real when a cTrader reference field is blank.Matching on several fields at once when there's no common reference.
Confidence
How sure the system is about a result, 0–100%. A guide for the reviewer — the model's certainty, not a guarantee.The model's certainty — not a guarantee.
Normalize canonical model
Convert each system's different format into one common shape so they can be compared. Each export names things differently; we read them all into one model.We read three different file formats into one common shape.
Value date
The date the money is actually effective for the account — which can differ from when the row was entered. Explains a lot of timing gaps.The date the money is effective — explains timing gaps.
Settlement lag T+1 / T+2
The money settles one or two business days after the request — a normal delay, not a loss. (Not shown as a label in the current demo — included so you can explain a "timing" match.)Settles one or two business days later — a normal delay.
Age · Owner · Status
For each break: how long it's been open (age), who's resolving it (owner), and where it stands (open / investigating / escalated / resolved). (Part of the full breaks-queue workflow — the module map — not the live matching demo.)How old the mismatch is, who owns it, and its state.
Escalated
A mismatch serious enough that a senior person or operator must review it now. (A status in the full system / module map.)Bumped up — needs higher-priority human review.
Hold pending investigation
Money we can't yet match is parked and traced before any action — not lost, just held until explained. (The formal term is a "suspense" account.)Held until we trace it — not lost, just parked.
Audit log / resolution note
A timestamped record of every match, break, and who-resolved-what-and-why — the trail an auditor asks for. (In the full system / module map.)Who did what, when, and why — the auditor's trail.

ADVANCED possible modules — real, but to be confirmed against your workflow

Exposure / unhedged
The broker's net open market risk — the B-book position that isn't covered by an A-book hedge. This is risk/dealing-desk work, not back-office reconciliation — a separate module, only if you want it.The broker's own net market risk — a separate risk module.
A-book / B-book
Whether the broker passes a client's trade out to the market (A-book) or takes the other side itself and holds the risk (B-book).Pass the trade to the market, or hold the risk yourself.
LP (liquidity provider)
Where A-book trades are hedged — the broker's counterparty in the market.Where hedged trades go — the broker's counterparty.
FIX / drop-copy
FIX is a standard real-time trading feed; a drop-copy is a read-only copy of every fill. A genuine production upgrade later — not wired up in the demo.A live trade feed — a real upgrade later, not connected yet.
Client-money segregation
Keeping client funds in a separate bank account from the firm's own money — a CySEC licence rule, reconciled daily. A high-value module, but built to your exact regulatory template, never assumed.Daily proof client money is kept separate — built to your template.
IB / rebate
An Introducing Broker (affiliate) paid a volume-based commission. Reconciling what's owed vs what was actually paid — depends on your commission model.Making sure partner commissions paid match what was earned.
Swap / rollover
Overnight interest charged on positions held past end of day. Must match what was actually charged on the platform.Overnight interest — must match what was charged.
Nostro MT940 / camt.053
Your own bank account (one per currency); MT940 (legacy) and camt.053 (modern) are the two bank-statement file formats we'd read.Your bank account and its statement formats — we read both.
Audit pack
A one-click bundled export of a period's reconciliations, breaks and sign-offs for an auditor. A deliverable we'd build, not an industry standard.One-click bundle of everything an auditor would ask for.
Maker-checker / four-eyes
One person acts, a different person approves — mandatory on any change that moves money, so no single person controls it.One person acts, a second approves — no single point of control.
AML velocity
An anti-money-laundering signal: an unusual burst of payments on one account in a short window. This is a separate compliance system from reconciliation — a possible add-on, not part of the core.An unusual burst of payments on one account — a separate AML module.
